How homeowners discover contractors

Across every major study of residential renovation behavior, personal referrals are the dominant first touchpoint — not Google. Homeowners in the $1M+ project range are even more reliant on trusted networks: the financial and disruption stakes are too high to risk on an unknown contractor found through search. The site's job, for Ashbury Construction, is validation — not discovery.

Primary discovery channel by study — % of homeowners
Referrals: 53–79% across studies. Online search: 25–62%. Prior relationship: 30% (Houzz).

Generational Contractor Hiring Behavior — CII Advisors
CII Advisors  ·  2024  ·  Homeowner trends for contractors
Nuance
Younger homeowners — Millennials and younger Gen X — increasingly rely on verified third-party reviews over word-of-mouth. The referral-dominant pattern skews toward Baby Boomers and older Gen X, who represent the primary buyers in the $1M+ project tier. As Pacific Heights buyers skew younger, Yelp and Google Reviews become a meaningful gap for Ashbury Construction.
What this means for Ashbury Construction
The research is consistent across every source: referrals drive discovery, and the website validates the decision. Ashbury Construction's current referral network — built through architect relationships and completed project quality — is the real lead engine. The site's job is to confirm what a referred prospect already suspects: that Ashbury Construction is the right contractor for a high-stakes, high-value project.
Finding confirmed
Referrals dominate discovery. Every major source confirms referrals are the dominant first touchpoint — especially for higher-income homeowners in the Boomer and older Gen X bracket, which is Ashbury Construction's core buyer segment for $1M–$5M projects.
Critical implication
The website is the second step — and it's not optional. 91% of homeowners check online before committing, even after receiving a referral. The current site creates risk at this validation point: sparse portfolio, passive forms, and no visible social proof can cause a warm referral to go cold.
SF market context
San Francisco is the highest-concentration GC market in the country. 54% of renovating SF homeowners hired a general contractor in 2024 — nearly double the national average. The demand for exactly what Ashbury Construction offers is concentrated and active in this market.
Emerging risk
Younger Pacific Heights buyers behave differently. Millennial homeowners increasingly rely on Yelp, Google Reviews, and Houzz profiles rather than personal referrals. Ashbury Construction's current review absence is a liability — not just a missed opportunity — for this segment of the market.
