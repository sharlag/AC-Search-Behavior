What this means for Ashbury Construction

The statement holds. Every major source confirms referrals are the dominant first touchpoint — especially for higher-income homeowners in the Boomer and older Gen X bracket, which is Ashbury Construction's core buyer for $1–5M projects.
The website is the second step, not optional. 91% of homeowners check online before hiring, even when referred. The site validates the referral — or kills it. Ashbury Construction's current site creates risk at this handoff point.
SF is the highest-GC-hiring metro in the country. 54% of renovating SF homeowners hired a general contractor in 2024 — nearly double the national average. The market for exactly what Ashbury Construction does is concentrated and active here.
Younger buyers are different. Millennial homeowners in Pacific Heights increasingly rely on Yelp, Google Reviews, and Houzz reviews rather than personal referrals. This is where Ashbury Construction's review absence is a liability — not just missed opportunity.
